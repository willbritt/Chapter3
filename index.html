Chapter 3 Essay

Chapter 3 in Cairo's book is all about the message that a visualization presents to its audience and the how close that message is to the absolute truth. According to Cairo, designers should focus on creating models that are not needlessly complex and that are hard to misinterpret by the viewer. All included information should be necessary for the message, and it should not be oversimplified. This allows the subject being conveyed to be more clarified and less likely to be misinterpeted. Cairo also mentions common traits/patterns that humans have that lead to mistakes by designers. These traits include: the patternicity bug, the storytelling bug, and the confirmation. Each one of these ideas can lead to incorrect judgements about different ideology and phenomenon. A designer's ability to overcome these tricks played by the mind can improve their chances of creating a visual, that is not necessarily perfect by any measure, but good enough to deliver an accuarte and meaningful messgae.

I am referring to instances of clickbait on the internet. Like Cairo referenced, intriguing headlines can really attract interest from readers. In the situation that I am referring to in particular, false headlines could be used positively if they were apart of links to important truthful information such as our current enviornmntal crisis. Using clickbait to more or less trick individuals into reading valueable information could be an interesting strategy choice by people trying to get valueable information out to the world.
